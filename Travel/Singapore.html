Singapore

I've been living between Dubai and Singapore for about a year now, and I must say, Singapore has really been a marvel. It's efficient public transport system, accessibility, walkability, and overall design, as someone who's lived in Dubai my whole life, it isn't the flashiness that gets me but the care and thoughtfullness that went into making the city livable for everyone that made me love it.

Architecture

One of the things I was quite fascinated by in Singapore was the architecture. The blend of modern skyscrapers with traditional buildings creates a unique skyline.

I really appreciated that skyscrapers were limited to the central city areas. As someone that complains about light pollution, the stars were very visible at night, which is something I rarely see!

Getting Around

I also loved that it's really walkable, the greenery and pedestrian paths were really well integrated, so are cycle lanes. Fair amount of shaded areas for when it rains! and I love how the pathways are surrounded by trees for extra shading from the sun.

I don't know how to explain how impressed I am with how disability friendly everything is. Ramps, elevators, braille signs, tactile paving, audible crosswalks, and so much more. It's one of the main reasons I can see myself living in Singapore someday, I can see myself growing old yet keeping my independence and self-sufficiency for longer.

The MRT system is also super efficient and easy to use. I never had to wait long for a train or busand the Apple Maps integration made it effortless. Accurate arrival times, clear routes, and no random app to download and forget about. Living in Dubai, it surprised me that this level of integration isn’t more common.
